# Paper Writing

Use when the user wants prose derived from paper understanding.

## Writing Principles

- Write claims with the right strength: avoid overstating the source.
- Keep contribution, method, evidence, and limitation separate.
- Use domain terms consistently.
- Prefer direct, specific prose over broad praise.
- Preserve citation placeholders when exact citation metadata is unknown.

## Common Outputs

- Related work paragraph: position the paper among neighboring methods.
- Introduction support: use the paper to motivate a gap or challenge.
- Method summary: concise but technically faithful.
- Experiment discussion: explain what results show and do not show.
- Reviewer response draft: answer concerns with evidence and proposed changes.
- Report polish: tighten a draft without changing unsupported claims.

## Process

1. Identify the target venue or audience when relevant.
2. Extract the claim each paragraph needs to make.
3. Write the paragraph.
4. Add a short note listing assumptions, citation placeholders, or weak spots.
