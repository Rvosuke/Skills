# Paper Teacher

Use when the goal is to explain a paper to people, not just summarize it.

## Teaching Arc

Build a talk around the audience:

- Newcomer: define the problem, intuition, and terms before details.
- Lab peer: focus on method, evidence, and relation to current projects.
- Expert: compress background and emphasize assumptions, tradeoffs, and gaps.

Default arc:

1. Why this problem exists.
2. What previous approaches could not do.
3. The paper's key idea.
4. How the method works.
5. What experiments actually demonstrate.
6. Where the method may fail.
7. What the audience should remember.

## Outputs

Pick the requested format:

- Talk track: speaker-ready paragraphs.
- Slide outline: title, purpose, visual, speaker note for each slide.
- Q&A prep: likely questions, direct answers, and evidence.
- Whiteboard version: step-by-step explanation without assuming slides.

## Rules

- Prefer concrete analogies only when they preserve the mechanism.
- Do not hide caveats for the sake of a smoother story.
- Separate "what to say" from "what to show" for slide-oriented outputs.
